Associate Productivity & Reviews

From WikiPortal, the free encyclopedia for all things DM Portal

This article is about associate productivity and reviews. For other related topics, see Reports (disambiguation).

This is a DM Portal Processor Review

DM Portal Associate Reviews, come in three different variations (Individual Contributor, Processor, and Lead), and provide the basis for merit increase at the end of each fiscal year. Reviews are delivered bi-monthly (every other month) with the first yearly review taking place in April for the months of February and March. A schedule for all review-related activities can be found on the DM Calendar.

Reviews are not provided to our contingent labor as the feedback given to this group is solely provided by representatives from each agency.

Leadership compliance with regard to Associate Review delivery is imperitive as we are providing feedback to our associates on areas of improvement and, even more important, areas where they execute well.

Review Reports

DM Portal Review Leader Form

This is the form located within DM Portal at 006-Review > Associate Reviews (edit), and will be used to view, modify, and print Associate Reviews by leaders.

As this data is very sensitive, a Portal User must be granted access to review data by the Morse Road Data Management Team. Should you encounter an issue where no data is populating in the form, you are likely not on the list of users given access An email to MGLData@lb.com will fix this issue.

Associate Review override scenarios arrise when a modification is required for a review after the review delivery period (1st week after the fiscal month ends). We require that any issue/request includes the associate's Manager and HR Partner. The additional approval process was implemented to create more conversation around a review that could have already been delivered to an associate. Our goal is to never change a review value after a review period is completed, but exceptions do arrise (ex. a poor day of productivity not toggled off, errant accuracy data, safety point updates, attendance point updates, procedural error updates, etc.)

Processor Review

The most common type of Associate Review is the Processor Review shown to the right. A "processor" would be defined as any DC hourly associate performing on-standard labor on a consistent basis (consistency to be determined by leadership).

A Processor Review will include the follow areas of assessment:
- Productivity - This section is an overall percent of an associates productive work for the fiscal month. The calculation is defined as Minutes Expected/Actual Minutes with any productive data deemed errant being adjusted through the Associate Tasks Form.
- Ratings - Exceeds > 110%, Meets 109.9%-90%, Does Not Meet < 90%
- YTD rating is determined by the YTD Expected Minutes/YTD Actual Minutes.
- Please keep in mind that this is a weighted percent.
- Quality - This section is an overall percent of units correct/units checked during an audit.
- Ratings - Exceeds >= 99.50% , Meets >= 97.00% and <= 99.49%, Does Not Meet < 97.00%
- Procedural Errors will also be shown in this section. For more information please see Procedural Errors.
- Teamwork - This is a subjective rating entered by the associate's immediate leader based upon
- Attendance - This section will show the assoiate's point total during the fiscal month as well as a running total in the YTD column.
- Ratings - Meets < 11 points YTD, Does Not Meet>= 11 points.
- An associate cannot currently Exceed in the attendance category.
- Safety - This section will show the assoiate's point total during the fiscal month as well as a running total in the YTD column.
- Ratings - Meets < 4 points YTD, Does Not Meet>= 4 points YTD.
- An associate cannot currently Exceed in the safety category.
- Comments - This section is to be filled out by the associate's immediate leader outlining the entire review with additional talking points if any are deemed necessary.

Once the review is completed for the associate a signiture from both, the associate and the leader, will be required on the physical copy to be turned into HR. It is also a requirement that the "Sign-off" button be pressed to indicate an electronic submission of the review. It is also common practice for a leader to provide an associate with a copy of the review.

Individual Contributor (IC) Review

The Individual Contributor (IC) Review is reserve for those hourly associates who are in a clerical position or perform off-standard work on a consistent basis (consistency to be determined by leadership).

An IC Review will include the follow areas of assessment:
- Technical Skills & Abilities - This is a subjective rating given to the associate by their immediate leader based on the demonstration of the functional/technical knowledge and skills to perform their job at a high level of accomplishment. Producing accurate work while being able to work independently seeking direction when appropriate. Proactively addresses issues and has a strong sense of urgency.
- An associate cannot currently Exceed in this category despite the Broad Street campus allowing an Exceeds rating until 2019. It was eliminated to ensure a fair opportunity to Exceed between all review types.
- Organization, Time Management & Dependability - This is a subjecive rating to be entered by the immediate leader based on the effective/efficient use of time, setting priorities and concentrating efforts accordingly. Able to balance multiple tasks and seeks direction when unsure how to proceed.
- Ratings - Exceeds , Meets, Does Not Meet
- Communication Skills & Customer Focus - This is a subjective rating entered by the associate's immediate leader based upon the ablity to write/verbalize clearly and in a concise manner at all levels to positively impact workflow. Regularly provides thoughtful suggestions to improve efficiency and overall job effectiveness. Goes above and beyond to exceed the expectations of internal and external customers.
- Teamwork - This is a sbujective section to be entered by the immediate leader of the associate with criteria below.
- Ratings - Exceeds would be an associate who overwhelmingly demonstrates a commitment to our values with consistent respect, positive work behaviors, flexibility, and active involvement.
- Attendance - This section will show the assoiate's point total during the fiscal month as well as a running total in the YTD column.
- Ratings - Meets < 11 points YTD, Does Not Meet>= 11 points.
- An associate cannot currently Exceed in the attendance category.
- Safety - This section will show the assoiate's point total during the fiscal month as well as a running total in the YTD column.
- Ratings - Meets < 4 points YTD, Does Not Meet>= 4 points YTD.
- An associate cannot currently Exceed in the safety category.

Once the review is completed for the associate a signiture from both, the associate and the leader, will be required on the physical copy to be turned into HR. It is also a requirement that the "Sign-off" button be pressed to indicate an electronic submission of the review. It is also common practice for a leader to provide an associate with a copy of the review.

Monthly Report

The Monthly Report can be run by clicking the "Monthly Report" button located on the main Leadership Review form.

This report will display the fiscal monrth selected in the drop-down on the main leadership review form by week with detailed job function productivity.

The YTD productivity data will also be displayed up to the month selected in the drop-down mentioned above.

All accuracy data collected will show on this report by week similarly to the productivity information.

Safety/Attendance data will be displayed at the bottom.

The intent of the report is to use as a discussion tool when providing feedback to an associate regarding productivity (training period or review end discussions) along with the other pertinent data mentioned.

The YTD value may not match the value displayed on the associate review depending in the time the report is being run.

Traffic Light

The Traffic Light Report was designed to show the breakdown of associate productivity by job function for a specific review month.

The intent is to give a reccomendation based on data for any UPH adjustments that may need to take place for the current month of the next month.

We do not allow UPH adjustments for months that have already been completed as this would have an impact on reviews that have already been delivered or signed-off.

This report is not intended to be posted or shown to associates.

1. Shows the associate name.

2. Shows the fiscal month being observed.

3. Shows the specific job function being observed.

4. Shows the tier based on your selections for 'Offset Value'.

The offset value is the deviation from the current UPH/LM setting (ex. UPH/LM set to 100 in UPH Monthly with an offset of 10 would then show UPH/LM calculations IF the setting was 110, 120, 90, and 80).

5. The current dispersion of associate productivity.

Associate Prod Misc Portal Forms

Tiering Form

The intent of the Tiering Form is to allow DC leaders to allot associates more time to complete tasks (ex. New hires, cross-training, associates who do not perform job function often, return from LOA, etc.).

A tier can be assigned from the 5 days prior to the current date and infinitely into the future.

Tier levels are determined by building leadership and can be different between buildings.

L1 - 30 days (date range will be automatically determined once a start date is entered)

Portal will automatically add L2, L3, and Reg after you have entered the L1 tier.

L2 - 30 days (date range will be automatically determined once a start date is entered)

If you skip L1, Portal will automatically add L3 and Reg after you have entered the L2 tier.

L3 - 30 days (date range will be automatically determined once a start date is entered)

If you skip L2, Portal will automatically add Reg after you have entered the L3 tier.

Reg - the default expectation level for any associate not on a tier program.

Associates can be tiered individually or as a group of associates by selecting the desired associates from the associate list on the left side of the form.

Once you have selected your desired associate/s, you will select a start date (end date will be automatically calculated and not able to be edited).

A job function or group of job functions can then be selected in the column at the far right of the form.

UPH Monthly

The UPH Monthly form is to be used by DC leaders to assign UPH/LM target values for each job function for each fiscal month.

The default value for every function is 10,000 with the intent being a group of associates performing poorly will alert a leader to adjust the UPH.

A UPH cannot be adjusted for a month that is already completed.

For assistance with UPH goals, please see the section on Traffic Light Reporting.

Adjust Activity Prod

The Adjust Activity Prod form is to be used sparingly as the impacts of creating an adjustment could effect a large group of associates.

The form is intended to be used when a large associate population requires an immediate productivity adjustment (ex. turning off prod for an entire day due to a system issue).

You are able to select a single date or a date range and use additional filters to select a department/activity(job function).

Associate Tasks

The Associate Tasks form is one you will want to be familiar with as it will be one of the most widely used tools when discussing associate productivity.

The intent of the form is to allow leaders the ability to turn productivity on/off by job function for a specific date (more granular then the Adjust Activity Prod form).

The leader can also enter 'adjustments' that will allot more time for an associate to complete a task. This section was designed to be used by LM Clericals for entering Time Credits (see LM/PE).

Associate Productivity Reports

Daily Posting Report

The Daily UPH Posting Report can be used for both UPH and LM job functions in our distribution centers.

The report can only be run for a 7-day Sunday through Saturday time frame which we have limited the date range function on the reports form to enforce this limitation.

The posting is typically posted once a week (Tuesdays) to allow productivity adjustments to be reflected accurately.

Posting Report Labels

1. Shows the job function for which the data is represented in the day of the week columns as well as the weekly column.

2. Shows the UPH/LM goal. The default value for this is 10,000 (the intent is to alert leaders through low productivity numbers that a UPH/LM adjustment needs made in UPH Monthly Form).

3. Shows the Kronos ID of the associate. A version of the report that has the name of the associate is available; however, we do not recommend posting any report with an associate's name.

4. Shows the WTD performance of the job function in box 1.

5. Shows the WTD performance for all functions performed by an associate.

6. Shows the MTD performance for all functions performed by an associate.

7. Shows the YTD performace for all functions performed by and associate.

Associates With DNM

The Associates With DNM Report was designed to quickly show the associates (by Supervisor) who have a 'Does Not Meet' rating on their Bi-monthly Review.

This report would be useful in a meeting discussing associate trends and the Traffic Light Report.

1. Shows the Supervisor and Associate name.

2. Shows the area of the reivew where the 'Does Not Meet' rating was assessed.

Review Data & Calculations

UPH Calculations

A UPH (Units Per Hour) calculation is applied in our non-LM supported buildings (DC1, DC2, DC3 Upper). At the beginning of each month a UPH standard (Portal 006-Review > UPH Monthly) is set by the leadership team according to the Traffic Light reporitng for the previous month which will outline your trending UPH for a given function for a given review period. An associate processing units in a given function will be cross-punched into a corresponding Kronos code that will allow the data team to capture the 'actual' amount of time an associate spent in the aforementioned function.

LM Calculations

PE% = (SAM/Tier)/(OSDL+UIL-Adj. OSDL)
-The associate Tier is set on the UPH Monthly Form or the Associate Tier form (see UPH Monthly OR Associate Tiering).
UTIL% = (OSDL+NSDL)/(Total Time-Breaks)
-
EFF% = SAM/(Total Time-Breaks)
