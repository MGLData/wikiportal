Definitions

ASN-Advanced Shipping Notice-a document that provides detailed information about a pending delivery. The purpose of an ASN is to notify the customer when shipping occurs and provide physical characteristics about the shipment so the customer can be prepared to accept delivery.

ASRS-Automated Storage & Retrieval System-a material handling sysytem implemented in DC2 and DC6 that brings finished goods to an associate from reserve automatically instead of an associate traveling to the finished goods eliminating the need for excess travel and replenishment labor.

ATS-Associate Technology Services-team responsible for desktop services and various other IT-related troubleshooting. Extension 57911 when dialing internally.

BBW-Bath & Body Works-an American retailer under the L Brands (formerly Limited Brands) umbrella, along with Victoria's Secret. It was founded in 1990 in New Albany, Ohio and has since expanded across 6 continents. In 1997, it was the largest bath shop chain in the United States of America. It specializes in shower gels, lotions, fragrance mists, perfumes, creams, candles, and home fragrances.

BBWD-Bath & Body Works Direct-the online designation for Bath & Body Works distribution.

BOL-Bill of Lading-is a document issued by a carrier (or their agent) to acknowledge receipt of cargo for shipment. Although the term historically related only to carriage by sea, a bill of lading may today be used for any type of carriage of goods. A comprehensive article on Bill of Lading Bills of lading are one of three crucial documents used in international trade to ensure that exporters receive payment and importers receive the merchandise. The other two documents are a policy of insurance and an invoice. Whereas a bill of lading is negotiable, both a policy and an invoice are assignable. In international trade outside the United States, bills of lading are distinct from waybills in that the latter are not transferable and do not confer title. Nevertheless, the UK Carriage of Goods by Sea Act 1992 grants "all rights of suit under the contract of carriage" to the lawful holder of a bill of lading, or to the consignee under a sea waybill or a ship's delivery order.

A bill of lading must be transferable, and serves three main functions:

-it is a conclusive receipt, i.e. an acknowledgement that the goods have been loaded; and

-it contains or evidences the terms of the contract of carriage; and

-it serves as a document of title to the goods, subject to the nemo dat rule

G2P-Goods To Person-another name for ASRS.

GOH-Garment On Hanger-special shipments of goods that require being shipped/storred on a hanger (ex. suits). This operation is located in DC3 upper.

GTP-Goods To Person-another name for ASRS0.

LM-Labor Management-a web solution developed by Manhattan Associates (also see PkMS) that tracks associate productivity in OF1, DC7, DC8, DC5, DC4, SB1, and SB2. This system is very accurate at measuring associate travel time throughout the warehouse and has many internal views that assist leaders in monitoring warehouse performance while providing timely/accurate feedback to associates.

LOA-Leave Of Absence-an extended leave from the business approved by HR and Management.

LPN-License Plate Number-a number assigned to a case or pallet used to track the movement of goods throughout the warehouse by our warehouse management system (also see PkMS).

MGT-Mast Global Technology-the technology branch of Mast Global.

MOOS-Marked Out Of Stock-a process performed in DC2 and DC3 to receive, store, and ship product that did not sell in Victoria's Secret stores. The entirety of this operation is carried out through MR Portal.

MRP-Morse Road Portal-the first development of DM Portal. The primary use for MR Portal is the MOOS operation.

NSDL-Non-Standard Direct Labor-labor designation for fuctions that are required to ship product out of a building but are unable to be assigned a productivity standard due to how many variables are present (ex. box making).

NYCO-New York & Company-a company processed through DC3 upper. This is part of the RTW (also see Retail Winds) family of brands.

OSDL-On-Standard Direct Labor-a labor disignation for the amount of time that an associate spent performing a function (also referred to as 'Actual Time').

PE-Performance Excellence OR Percent Effective-a calculation for determining the effectiveness percent at which an associate performe. SAM/(OSDL+UIL)

PKMS-Pick Ticket Management System-the warehouse management system utilized by all of the Mast Global warehouses. This system stores inventory information and assigns tasks for associates to perform.

PTO-Paid Time Off-the amout of paid time an associate is allocated based on duration of service to the company.

RDC-Regional Distribution Center-smaller, 3rd party distribution centers around the United States utilized by Mast Global to process volume and deliver products to our customers in a more timely manner. The locations are New Jersey, Atlanta, Columbus (Vista), and California.

RFID-Advanced Shipping Notice, .

RTW-Retail Winds-the parent company of New York & Company.

SAM-Standard Allowable Minutes-a labor designation assigned by LM for the amount of time an activity SHOULD have taken an associate to complete. This value is determined by extensive engineered labor studies.

SB1-Shipping Building 1-shipping building located on Morse Road.

SB2-Shipping Building 2-shipping building located on Broad Street (attached to DC7).

UDIL-User Defined Indirect Labor-a labor designation for the time spent by an associate performing functions that do not directly assist in the movement of goods through a building (ex.cleaning).

UIL-Unapprove Indirect Labor-a labor designation assigned to an associate whose time cannot be accounted for while performing a function (often times referred to as 'gap time').

UPH-Units Per Hour-the goal set by leaders for the amount of units an associate should process for each hour performing a function. This can be adjusted during a review period, but cannot be adjusted for a month that is already complete (ex. a user could not adjust the UPH for November if the current month is December).

VAS-Value Added Services-services performed on finished goods necessary for their sale in stores (ex. reticketing due to a price change or adding stickers to product).

VM-Virtual Machine-an environment being used by an associate on a computer that does not have a physcial machine (often identified by the use of Wyse Boxes). This allows MGT (Mast Global Technology) to more efficiently manage our network.
